How it works

Three ways to connect

Lean In Connect has three types of communities — each built for a different kind of relationship. Here's what they're for and how to find yours.

Circle

Your closest peer group

A Circle is a small group of 8–12 women who meet monthly to support each other's careers and lives. It's peer-led — no expert needed — and uses free curriculum from Lean In to guide each meeting. Think of it as your personal board of advisors who actually know you.

8–12 members Meets monthly Peer-led Free curriculum Private & trusted
Find a Circle
Network

Your city or industry community

A Network is a larger community organized around where you live or what you do — like "Lean In Bay Area" or "Women in Healthcare." Networks host events, share opportunities, and are often where people find their Circle. You can belong to more than one.

Hundreds to thousands of members City or industry based Events-focused Open to join
Find a Network
Group

A discussion space for any topic

Groups are online communities built around shared topics — salary negotiation, career pivots, working parenthood, design, and thousands more. They're topic-based and open to anyone on the platform. Jump in, ask a question, share what you know.

Topic-based Online & async Open discussion Join multiple Free to join
